About me

Ana Kajaia is a fourteen-year-old artist from Kutaisi, Georgia.

Ana is a student at Public School No. 31, where the rhythms of everyday school life run quietly alongside a stubborn, unshakeable love for drawing and painting. From an early age, colour, line, and the telling of stories on paper came naturally to her — not as homework, but as a place where she could think out loud and make sense of the world around her.

For Ana, creativity is not simply a pastime — it is a way of seeing. Her work brings imagination and feeling together: scenes and characters that carry mood, memory, and the small, easily overlooked details she notices in daily life. She enjoys experimenting with ideas, styles, and atmospheres, building her own voice gradually and deliberately, without rushing to imitate anyone else. Each piece is a step in a longer, patient conversation with herself.

What Ana wants most is for her art to feel honest. Her works are not merely pleasing to look at — they are meant to hold something: a sense of wonder, an emotion, a clearly personal way of perceiving the world. It is this vision that she wishes to share with those who value original art — collectors, friends of the arts, and anyone curious about the next generation of painters emerging from Georgia.
